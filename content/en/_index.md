2.  Context Engineering is now the cutting-edge trend in AI, perhaps signaling the end of the "prompt engineering" era. A recently published, significant [AI News: Major Review Paper](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.13334) officially defines this emerging field. It systematically explores how to "feed" information to LLMs more efficiently and accurately, covering a range of advanced "feeding" techniques from RAG to complex multi-agent systems. After a deep dive into over 1300 related studies, the paper uncovers a thought-provoking phenomenon: today's AI models are top-tier at "understanding human language," but when it comes to "speaking human language"‚Äîespecially generating complex long-form content‚Äîthey still act like clumsy beginners. üß† Bridging this massive gap between "knowing and doing" will be one of the core challenges for AI's future development.

3.  TAIL (Turing MAchine Imitation Learning), a novel method, has been proposed by researchers, offering a rather philosophical answer to a crucial question: How can AI learn true logical reasoning instead of just rote memorization from massive datasets? The answer is to teach it to think fundamentally, like the granddaddy of computers‚Äîthe Turing Machine. By guiding LLMs to imitate every atomic step of a Turing Machine executing tasks, this method significantly boosts the model's length generalization ability when tackling ultra-long sequence problems. It's like teaching students the underlying logic and first principles of problem-solving, rather than just drilling answers into them. This work might just be lighting up a "back to basics" path for solving the reasoning bottlenecks LLMs currently face. üí° [AI News: Paper Here](https://arxiv.org/abs/2507.13332).
